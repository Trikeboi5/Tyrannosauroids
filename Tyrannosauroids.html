Tyrannosauroids

Depiction of Dryptosaurus aquilunguis

Tyrannosauroids are Therapods which arrived in the Jurassic period bearing quite a bit of feathering and later dominated in the Northern Hemisphere of the Cretaceous growing bigger in size and slowly losing some of their feathers.

Classification

Tyrannosauroids are divided into 2 main families, the smaller crested Proceratosaurids and the larger ferocious Tyrannosaurids(and the primitive family Dryptosauridae which only includes Dryptosaurus and Cryptotyrannus and quite a few not in any more specific group such as Eotyrannus, Dilong, Xiangguanlong, etc.)

Proceratosaurids

Depiction of Guanlong wucaii

Proceratosaurids vary a little in size, with most being a little bigger than a turkey while a few being medium sized therapods

Proceratosaurids have the feature of having a crest on the top of their head which might have been used for mating display

Proceratosaurids had 3 fingers on their arms and would have had quite a lot amount of feathering

Tyrannosaurids

Depiction of Gorgosaurus libratus

Tyrannosaurids go from medium-sized to some of the largest Therapods to ever rule the land with less feather found on the large ones such as Tyrannosaurus which might have even had no feathering

Tyrannosaurids are characterized by the crests that go over their eyes and having 2 fingers on each of their tiny arms and chonkier bodies than their relatives

Tyrannosaurids were the apex predators of each of their ecosystem with relatively shorter snouts compared to most other therapods and big, rounder slightly serated teeth perfect for their crushing bites
